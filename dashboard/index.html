Headline results — continuous out-of-sample (parameters never saw the test data)

Read this first. All "OOS" numbers below come from walk-forward testing: parameters are re-optimized every 28 days using only past data, then applied to the next unseen 28 days, simulated continuously (positions and equity carry across refits). Numbers are inflated by full-equity compounding and exclude funding fees. 35 of 36 leveraged no-stop configurations were liquidated at least once in 19 months of OOS testing. The one survivor is shown below — it survived stress tests (1-bar delay, 2.5x fees) but its OOS window contains no event like March 2024 (+34% adverse run), and as 1-of-36 it carries survivorship risk. There is no configuration of "high leverage + no stop" that history certifies as safe.

Methods tested

Signal normalization
The original V5 entry thresholds are fixed price-%/absolute values, so trigger rates collapse ~10× in quiet markets. V6 z-scores the MACD signals against their own trailing 3-day volatility — trigger rates become regime-invariant. Also re-enables the dead cross-long subsystem and optionally blocks shorts in strong uptrends.
Regime methods
Per-bar market classification (causal, trailing percentiles): none (single set), vol3/vol3_7d (volatility terciles, 30d/7d memory), volume3 (dollar-volume terciles), trend3 (price vs 1-day EMA z-score), volXtrend9 (3×3 grid). Every parameter can differ per regime.
Window study
Training window: 42d / 91d / 182d rolling vs expanding-all, refit every 28 days. Result: every rolling-window leveraged config died; expanding windows won for spot too. Short-memory refits overfit the recent regime and get killed by the next one.
Safety model
Leveraged mode has NO stop-loss (your live style): exits only at profit targets; liquidation modeled per-trade at (1/leverage − 0.8%) adverse intrabar move; leverage capped at 10x; feasibility requires zero liquidations and mark-to-market drawdown ≤ 80% on training data. Spot mode: long-only, 1×, stops ON, 0.05% fees.

Stress tests — the leveraged survivor (v6 · lev · none · expanding)

Also verified: robust to fee doubling (0.06%/side → 91×) and to 0.10%/side (→ 47×). Median leverage the optimizer chose: 4.5× (range 1.2–9.9) — the 80% drawdown cap binds before the 10× cap does. Shorts were partially disabled by the optimizer in ~20% of folds (3m system) and ~57% (cross system). Untested: the OOS span (Nov 2024→Jul 2026) contains no March-2024-scale melt-up; survival of such an event is not certified.

Findings & honest caveats

  

    1. The Scalp strategy (VRVP+CVD+EMA) has no edge. Its own TradingView export loses −16% over 4 years. All 6 leveraged variants liquidated OOS; all 6 spot variants lost money OOS (best: −1.2%/mo). Its CVD/POC signals are also hypersensitive to volume-feed noise (only 94% bar-direction agreement between feeds). Recommendation: do not trade it.


    2. V6 leveraged/no-stop can print spectacular OOS numbers (126×/19mo) but survival is structural luck. The same strategy family died in 35 of 36 tested configurations. What actually protected the survivor: moderate leverage (~4.5×) chosen by the drawdown constraint, and monthly re-fitting on expanding history.


    3. V6 spot (long-only, 1×, stops on) is modest but real: +1.2–1.4%/mo OOS (~17%/yr) with 19% max drawdown, using volatility-tercile regimes.


    4. Longer training memory wins. Weeks-scale windows overfit; expanding (years) windows survived. Re-fit cadence of 28 days was used throughout.


    5. Baselines: your original V5 config at 8× with the stop ON compounded absurdly in backtest but ran within 2.3% of liquidation twice; with the stop OFF (your live setting) it was liquidated on 2024-03-14 on real MEXC data. The Scalp original loses at 1×.
